Quantum Security

What quantum risk means for encryption—and how Privana adds a defense-in-depth layer today.

What Are Quantum Computers?

Classical vs. Quantum

Classical computers use bits (0 or 1) and step through problems. Quantum computers use qubits that can be 0 and 1 at once, letting them explore many possibilities in parallel.

Translation: certain math problems that protect today’s encryption could become easier for sufficiently powerful quantum machines.

Classical Computer

Tries candidates one-by-one.

Quantum Computer

Explores many possibilities at once.

Why It Matters

Classical Crypto At Risk

RSA/ECDH-based schemes may become vulnerable to sufficiently powerful quantum computers.

Harvest-Now, Decrypt-Later

Data captured today could be stored and decrypted in the future. Long-lived secrets are most exposed.

Timeline Is Uncertain

Whether years or longer, the safe approach is to harden traffic now so it stays private later.

VPN Takeaway

Confidentiality shouldn’t expire. If encrypted traffic is stored today, it shouldn’t become readable tomorrow.

That’s why Privana adds an extra key layer on top of WireGuard—so even if the base key exchange were weakened in the future, your captured packets still face a second, independent barrier.
